How I Ended up at my Second Studio

The good things in life never last. Unfortunately, I had to leave the Tampa Jiu Jitsu studio despite the community I had found there because I moved across town -- and if you should know anything about me, it's that I hate sitting in traffic. So I began a search for another jiu jitsu studio. To my surprise, there were several reputable jiu jitsu studios in the Tampa Bay area. A sucker for convenience, I decided to check out the closest one to my new apartment.

Gracie Tampa turned out to be more intimidating than my last studio. ("Gracie" is a subset of Brazilian Jiu Jitsu that was founded by the legendary Gracie family). Firstly, Gracie Tampa is more oriented towards MMA competition than my previous studio. In fact, several of their top students had just participated in a tournament in Abu Dhabi when I arrived. I came from a school more focused on practical self-defense, so I had to change mindsets a little bit. Secondly, Gracie Tampa practices jiu jitsu without the gi. A gi is special martial arts attire that allows for many holds and chokes. It looks like a robe with baggy pants. So I also had to adjust my sparring style, abandoning my habitual use of a cross choke and getting used to the monkey grip rather than gripping a sleeve or pant leg.

Despite a lot of hiccups during my first few weeks at the new studio, I came to love Gracie Tampa as much as Tampa Jiu Jitsu. It turned out that Rob Kahn, the owner and sensei of Gracie Tampa, actually knew my former sensei Ryan because they both trained under Royce Gracie. Small world! I got accustomed to sparring without my gi, and I even bought a rashguard with a beautiful geisha ankle locking a demon printed on it. But like I said, all good things come to an end. I got busy with work, and I decided to take a break from jiu jitsu. I haven't practiced consistently since then. Hopefully I find time to start training again, because the benefits were abundant. I kept in good shape, but I also ended up making friends, having fun, and feeling more centered through the practice.
